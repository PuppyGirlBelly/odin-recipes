Curry Recipe

Description

This is a basic curry recipe with some special ingredients that I like to use.

Ingredients

  • 1 Large Curry block (200-230g)
    • (Favorites include S&B curry, Vermont curry, and Golden Curry)
  • ~1L Carton of Campbell Pork Ramen Broth
  • 1-2lbs of pork - Cut sogigiri (そぎ切り), or cut at an angle.
  • 1 Bell Pepper - Cored and cut into squares
  • 1 Medium Onion (300-670g) - Cut into wedges, and caramelize.
  • 2 Carrots (190g) - Cut rangiri (乱切り), or rotated.
  • 4 Cloves of garlic - Grated/minced
  • 3 Yukon Gold Potatoes (432g) - Cut into 1.5-2cm cubes.
  • 2 Tbsp of a neutral oil

"Secret" Ingredients

(all are added with broth)

  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 1 Tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Worcestershire
  • 1 Tbsp Butter
  • 1 Tbsp Oyster Sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Rice Wine Vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp of Ketchup
  • 1 Tsp of Ground Black Pepper
  • Splash of sake

Directions

  1. Caramelize the onions with about 2 Tbsp of neutral oil under medium low-heat.
  2. Then fry the garlic in the remaining oil after the onions are caramelized until fragrant.
  3. Add the pork and cook until you don't see any pink left on the pieces.
  4. Add the carton of ramen broth to the broth and raise the heat to high to help it boil.
  5. As you're waiting for the broth to boil, add any appropriate secret ingredients to the broth.
  6. Add the potatoes, carrots, bell pepper, and other veggies that you make like to the broth
  7. Once it boils, lower the heat to a simmer and let it sit covered for 15-20 mintues. Until a knife easily pierces a carrot piece.
  8. Skim any scum and fat that has accumulated onto the surface with a fine mesh strainer.
  9. Remove from heat add curry blocks to the broth, and stir until they are fully dissolved and absorbed.
  10. Simmer once again for 5-10 minutes on medium-low heat to help thicken curry, and stir occasionally to prevent ingredients and roux from burning.
  11. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve with rice, noodles, or bread.
